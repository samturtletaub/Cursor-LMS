## Category map (talk tracks, not textbooks)

Buyers will use inconsistent language. Your job is to **translate claims into workflow outcomes**.

- **Autocomplete**: suggests the next tokens; great for local edits; limited “planning.”
- **Chat grounded in the repo**: Q&A + guided changes; depends on how well the tool can **retrieve** relevant context.
- **Multi-file edits**: applies a coherent change across files; success depends on validation (tests, types, review).
- **Agentic workflows**: the tool plans steps, uses tools, and iterates—**higher upside and higher governance need**.

<KeyTakeaway>
The category shift is from “help me write syntax” to “help me drive a change safely across the system.” That shift changes **who gets nervous** (security, quality, IP) and what proof you need (controls, auditability, enterprise policy).
</KeyTakeaway>

## Context windows (what to say on a call)

A model can only condition on a finite amount of text at once. In practice, teams care about:

- **How much of my repo can you reason about for this task?**
- **How do you retrieve the right slices** (search, embeddings, manual selection)?
- **How do you handle big diffs and long threads** without losing the plot?

You do not need to win a lecture on transformers. You need to show you understand **why large-codebase workflows are hard**.

## MCP (Model Context Protocol) — sales-credible framing

MCP is best described as **a standard way for an assistant to connect to tools and data sources** with explicit integration boundaries—useful language for enterprises that worry about ad-hoc plugins and ungoverned access.

<Callout title="Security translation">
MCP is an **integration story**: what is connected, who approves it, how access is scoped, and how activity is observable in enterprise controls.
</Callout>

## Common buyer questions you should expect

- “What data leaves our environment?”
- “Can we restrict models, agents, and integrations by policy?”
- “How do we prevent exfiltration via prompts, tools, or supply-chain dependencies?”
