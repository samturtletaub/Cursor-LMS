## Surfaces sellers should know

You should be able to orient a buyer in one minute:

- **Inline completion** accelerates local edits.
- **Chat** helps exploration, clarification, and guided changes.
- **Composer / agent-style flows** help multi-step changes—where governance matters most.
- **Background agents / automation adjacent workflows** can reduce toil when teams have clear guardrails.

<KeyTakeaway>
Your credibility jumps when you can connect a feature to **a repeatable engineering workflow** (onboarding, refactors, test fixes, migrations)—not a list of adjectives.
</KeyTakeaway>

## Enterprise controls (learn the vocabulary)

Expect Security/IT to ask about:

- **SSO / SAML** and directory integration
- **SCIM** for lifecycle management
- **Model access controls** and policy gates
- **MCP controls** (what integrations are allowed)
- **Privacy Mode / data handling** postures (describe accurately—avoid hand-waving)
- **Retention postures** such as **Zero Data Retention** where applicable (confirm current packaging and claims on Cursor’s trust materials before quoting as contractual facts)

<Callout title="Interview tip">
If you are unsure on a security detail, say so and point to **primary sources** (trust docs, security pages). Strategic sellers earn trust by being precise.
</Callout>

## Proof points: use them as prompts for discovery

Public marketing stats are useful **as hypotheses to validate** in-account:

- Where is the team trying to increase merge frequency?
- Where is review a bottleneck?
- What would “developer preference” mean in *this* org’s evaluation process?

Next: flashcards → quiz. Aim for 90%+ by learning the *definitions*, not memorizing marketing phrases verbatim.
